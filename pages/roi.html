Understanding ROI

Return on Investment (ROI) is a performance measure used to evaluate the efficiency or profitability of an investment. ROI tries to directly measure the amount of return on a particular investment, relative to the investment's cost.

How to Use ROI

  • Compare different investment opportunities
  • Evaluate past investment decisions
  • Set investment goals and benchmarks
  • Make data-driven investment choices

ROI Considerations

  • Consider the time period of the investment
  • Account for all costs, including fees and maintenance
  • Factor in the risk level of investments
  • Compare ROI with industry benchmarks
