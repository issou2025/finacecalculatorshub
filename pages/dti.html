Understanding DTI Ratio

Your debt-to-income ratio (DTI) is a key financial metric that compares how much you owe each month to how much you earn. Lenders use this ratio to measure your ability to manage monthly payments and repay debts.

What to Include in Monthly Debt

  • Mortgage or rent payments
  • Car loans
  • Student loans
  • Credit card minimum payments
  • Personal loan payments
  • Other debt obligations

DTI Ratio Guidelines

  • 43% or less: Maximum for most mortgages
  • 36% or less: Preferred by most lenders
  • 28% or less: Excellent debt management
  • 50% or more: Financial stress warning

Tips to Improve DTI

  • Pay down existing debt
  • Avoid taking on new debt
  • Increase your income
  • Refinance to lower payments
  • Create a debt payoff strategy
